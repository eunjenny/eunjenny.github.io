Q1: In the opening paragraph of her post, Seu asks you, the reader to join her to 'defamiliarize our tools.' What do you think she means by that? And how do you think that action relates to her use of the word poetry?

According to the article, "Let’s defamiliarize our tools and use them as a vehicle for contemplation." she says that she makes our tools defamiliarize and uses them as a means of meditation. The tools that we talk about here are the things that we do on a daily interface that we point at, swipe at, browse through, type in, and click on. They say that identifying the constraints of these tools can help us recognize their poetic potential. Technology comes to us as the use of tools. Continuous development and various kinds of technologies We think that the idea of these tools and the way in which we use technology, the elimination and thinking of functions can help us recognize our poetic potential. Literary works express different emotions and thoughts for each person who sees them. Like this, I think poetry is constantly capable of various kinds of interpretations and endings and means infinite possibilities. I think what she's trying to say in her writing is that she thinks that these tools help us to bring in new perspectives and potentials by feeling unfamiliar, so she means to defamiliarize our tools and use them as a vehicle for contemplation.
