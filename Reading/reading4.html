Q1: Why does Laurel Schwulst advocate for individual websites over social media? Do You agree with her? Please explain your answer.

According to the reading, Laurel Schwulst thinks especially pressing when an age of information overload and an increasingly commercialized web, artists of all types are the people to help. Artists can think expansively about what a website can be. Each artist should create their own space on the web, for a website is an individual act of collective ambition. I agree with her That we need individuals today rather than companies that will lead the future of the web. By creating their own website, artists can design their world, create their own works, and create their own. The social media we're using today feels very convenient and attractive, but we don't focus on our users, our happiness or our needs. They're focusing more on making advertising a priority than on users and generating their profits. So we can't say we're their subjects. From this perspective, it is very necessary for artists to create their own websites, exist as subjects and objects, and build their own identities and works, and I think it is very helpful for individuals to lead their own websites and find their own identity. It is very important to create your own space, and I think it is necessary for each artist to expand and develop their strength and power.
